01

Company & Mission

Why Tesla exists, and the trajectory that got it here.

The Mission

To accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy — not one product, but an entire ecosystem of generation, storage, and transport.

— Tesla, Inc.
1995 → Today

Two decades of acceleration

  1. 2003

    Tesla Motors founded with a single belief: EVs can beat gas cars.

  2. 2008

    The Roadster proves electric can be fast, desirable, and real.

  3. 2012

    Model S redefines the premium sedan and launches Supercharging.

  4. 2017

    Model 3 brings the mission to the mass market at scale.

  5. 2023

    Cybertruck enters production; the fleet crosses 5M cars built.

  6. 2026

    Robotaxi scales and Optimus moves from prototype to product.

Core Technology

Vertical Integration

One stack,
built in-house

  • Cells & packs — chemistry, structural batteries, the 4680.
  • Powertrain — motors, inverters, and 800-volt architecture.
  • Software — one OS, improved over the air, for the life of the car.
  • Silicon & AI — custom chips for inference and training.

Battery & Powertrain

Energy density, perfected

4680 Cell

A larger tabless cell — more energy, more power, lower cost per kWh.

Structural Pack

The battery is the chassis — fewer parts, more rigidity, lighter mass.

Drive Units

Compact permanent-magnet and induction motors, up to a tri-motor Plaid.

Gigafactory

The machine that builds the machine

Giga-casting replaces dozens of stamped parts with a single piece. Unboxed assembly builds sub-modules in parallel, then snaps them together.
